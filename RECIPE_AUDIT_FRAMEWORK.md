## Consensus Kitchen Cabinet: The Four Chefs

### 1. Jacques Pépin
- **Authority:** Classical French technique, knife skills, flavor foundations
- **Focus in TUB:** Sauces, vegetable prep, scaling, simplicity (does more with less)
- **Key Books:** "The Complete Pépin," "Essential Pépin"
- **Role:** The "why" behind technique — teaches principles, not just recipes

### 2. Thomas Keller
- **Authority:** Precision, timing, ingredient quality, haute cuisine standards
- **Focus in TUB:** Protein cooking (steak, fish, chicken), temperature control, plating
- **Key Books:** "The French Laundry Cookbook," "Ad Hoc at Home"
- **Role:** The "how perfectly" — exact temperatures, timing, finishing

### 3. Gordon Ramsay
- **Authority:** Speed, home-cook accessibility, flavor development, entertaining
- **Focus in TUB:** Quick weeknight meals, entertaining for groups, flavor over fussiness
- **Key Books:** "Gordon Ramsay's Home Cooking," "Cooking for Friends"
- **Role:** The "how practically" — shortcuts that don't sacrifice quality

### 4. Alice Waters
- **Authority:** Ingredient quality, seasonality, simplicity, respect for food
- **Focus in TUB:** Seasonal awareness, ingredient sourcing, respecting the raw material
- **Key Books:** "The Art of Simple Food"
- **Role:** The "why it matters" — ingredient selection, timing with seasons, honoring the source

## Validation Methodology

### For Each Recipe, We Validate:

#### 1. **Ingredient List Accuracy**
- [ ] Quantities realistic? (Is 1 tbsp of salt actually right, or should it be 1 tsp?)
- [ ] Ingredient choices reflect consensus? (Type of rice for risotto, cut of beef, pasta shape)
- [ ] Substitutions documented? (If chef disagrees on ingredient, note the alternative)
- [ ] Sourcing guidance clear? (Where to buy, what to look for, seasonal availability)

**Example checklist for Risotto Milanese:**
- Carnaroli rice: Yes (Pépin + Keller prefer it; Ramsay accepts Arborio as shortcut)
- Saffron amount: 2 pinches vs. 3? (Document consensus: 2 pinches = Keller/Pépin, 3 = Ramsay bolder)
- Stock type: Chicken vs. vegetable? (Consensus: chicken for richness, but vegetable works)
- Butter finish: 30g or 50g? (Document both approaches; indicate which for richness)

#### 2. **Technique Accuracy**
- [ ] Cooking method correct? (Is it really stovetop risotto, or should it start in oven?)
- [ ] Heat levels specified? (Medium-high vs. just "medium"?)
- [ ] Timing realistic? (Does risotto really take 18 minutes, or is that undershooting?)
- [ ] Crucial steps highlighted? (Which moment is "the point of no return"?)

**Example checklist for Cacio e Pepe:**
- Pasta water starch integration: Is "agitate vigorously" enough, or needs 1-2 minute rest?
- Cheese temp check: Must cheese be cold? (Keller: yes; Ramsay: room temp works if careful)
- Pepper blooming: Before or after pasta water? (Consensus: after, in the emulsion)
- Doneness: Full al dente or slightly softer for creaminess? (Document both camps)

#### 3. **Temperature & Timing**
- [ ] Internal temps for meat? (Steak: 52–54°C for medium-rare per Keller?)
- [ ] Pan temp for searing? (Oil smoking or just shimmering?)
- [ ] Simmer vs. boil distinctions? (Recipe says "simmer" — what does that mean numerically?)
- [ ] Rest times documented? (Steak 5 min? 10 min? Why?)

**Example checklist for Pan-Seared Steak:**
- Keller spec: Dry aged, 1.5 inches, 52–54°C center, 1 min/side sear, 5 min rest
- Ramsay spec: Any steak works, higher heat, cook to preference, rest 3–5 min
- Consensus: Internal temp is non-negotiable; rest time essential; sear time depends on thickness
- Documented variance: Ramsay prefers higher temp during rest (warm plate); Keller rests at room temp

#### 4. **Flavor Profile & Pairing**
- [ ] Expected flavor: Is it meant to be punchy or subtle?
- [ ] Seasoning levels: Salt/acid/fat ratios correct?
- [ ] Pairing suggestions: Wine, side, occasion — do they make sense?
- [ ] Variations documented: Lighter version? Richer version? Faster version?

**Example checklist for Thai Green Curry:**
- Expected heat level: Medium or hotter? (Consensus: medium for home cook, can adjust)
- Coconut milk ratio: Thin or thick sauce? (Keller/Pépin: thinner; Ramsay: creamier)
- Fish sauce amount: Enough to be savory or should it be subtle? (Consensus: present but not pungent)
- Variations: Chicken vs. shrimp? Vegetarian option? (Document all, note which is primary)

#### 5. **Educational Depth**
- [ ] "Why" explained, not just "what"? (Why does risotto need stirring? Starch release.)
- [ ] Common mistakes flagged? (Undercooked risotto? Broken emulsion in Cacio e Pepe?)
- [ ] Skill progression clear? (Is this a beginner recipe or intermediate?)
- [ ] Scaling logic documented? (How do we adjust for 4 servings vs. 1? Does timing change?)

## Consensus Rules

### When chefs agree (3+ out of 4): ✓ Canon
- No conflict. This is the standard.
- Example: All agree risotto needs stirring to release starch.

### When chefs split (2 vs. 2 or 2 vs. 1 vs. 1): Document both, favor majority
- Majority approach is primary (marked as consensus)
- Minority approach is documented as valid alternative
- Example: Ramsay likes higher heat/faster searing; Keller prefers lower heat/longer cook. Document both, favor Keller (2 votes: Keller + Pépin) as primary, note "Ramsay's approach works but requires more attention."

### When one chef uniquely disagrees: Note, but don't prioritize
- Example: Waters avoids butter in some sauces; others embrace it. Document her reasoning, but if 3 agree on butter, butter is canon. Note: "Waters' lighter approach: use olive oil + broth instead."

### Record dissent transparently
- "Pépin and Keller both favor 54°C doneness. Ramsay is comfortable up to 55°C. Consensus: 52–55°C is acceptable, with 54°C as the target."
- This teaches the bachelor *why* variations exist (different priorities: precision vs. flexibility).

## Quality Gates

Before a recipe is marked ✅ **Audited & Approved:**

1. **Ingredient list:** All quantities and types match consensus
2. **Technique:** All steps reflect consensus; no shortcuts that compromise quality
3. **Temperatures/timings:** Exact and justified (e.g., "54°C because this achieves medium-rare without drying the meat")
4. **Voice:** Still reads as "Warm Premium," not a copy-paste from any single chef
5. **Educational depth:** Explains *why* at every crucial step
6. **Scaling:** Documented how to cook for 1 vs. 4 servings
7. **Variations:** Documents valid alternatives (lighter, faster, richer)
