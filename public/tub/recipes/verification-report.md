## Recipes with Terminology Issues

### ⚠️ `smash-burgers-special-sauce`
**Issue**: Uses American terminology "ground beef"  
**Location**: `ing[0][0][1]` - "Freshly ground beef chuck (80/20 fat ratio), divided into 2 × 55g balls"  
**Required correction**: Replace "ground beef" with "beef mince"  
**Reason**: South African and UK English uses "beef mince" or "minced beef", not "ground beef" (North American term)  
**Source**: [Food terminology comparison](https://www.facebook.com/speakgoodenglishmovement/posts/minced-meat-or-ground-meat-from-pies-to-burgers-its-a-global-kitchen-staple-minc/1031043458730102/) confirming South Africa uses "mince" terminology

### ⚠️ `thai-green-curry`
**Issue**: Uses problematic term "kaffir lime leaves"  
**Locations**: Multiple instances throughout recipe  
**Required correction**: Replace all instances of "kaffir lime leaves" with "makrut lime leaves"  
**Reason**: "Kaffir" is a racial slur with offensive historical meanings. The culturally appropriate Thai name is "makrut lime" (from *Citrus hystrix*)  
**Source**: [Terminology guidance](https://www.foodandwine.com/news/whats-in-a-name-kaffir-lime-vs-makrut-lime) confirming "makrut lime" is the preferred, culturally appropriate Thai name
