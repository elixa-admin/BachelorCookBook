# TUB — Recipe Authoring Standard

> The canonical brief for every recipe in TUB. Apply to all new recipes and to
> retrofits of existing ones. Point authoring subagents here.

## Consensus-Driven Culinary Philosophy (PRIMARY — governs everything below)

The Ultimate Bachelor Cookbook is **not a collection of experimental recipes.** It is a curated library of the **best versions of the world's most loved meals**, adapted for South African tastes and home kitchens.

- **Identify the consensus before authoring.** Before generating any recipe, determine the recognised culinary consensus for that dish by analysing respected restaurant menus, award-winning chefs, classic cookbooks and highly-rated home recipes. Give particular weight to **leading South African restaurants (Johannesburg and Cape Town)** and to internationally recognised restaurants serving the same cuisine.
- **Synthesis, not invention.** Treat every recipe as a synthesis rather than an invention. Where **≈65% or more of credible sources** consistently agree on key ingredients, techniques, cooking order, temperatures, seasoning or finishing methods, **adopt that consensus as the foundation.** Depart from consensus only when there is a clear, evidence-based culinary improvement.
- **Proven SA favourites first.** Prioritise dishes proven in South African homes and restaurants — especially **steakhouse, Italian, Portuguese, South African, Cape Malay, Indian, Mediterranean, seafood, barbecue and modern comfort food.** Recipes should reflect what successful restaurants actually serve, while remaining practical for an enthusiastic home cook.
- **Polished restaurant classic, not novelty.** Focus on flavour development, proper technique, ingredient quality and repeatability. Use thoughtful chef-inspired upgrades only when they meaningfully enhance the dish without compromising accessibility.
- **Designed for the modern bachelor.** Generous, satisfying, protein-forward, visually impressive, suited to entertaining, and realistic to prepare after work or on weekends. Where appropriate, include optional **Health-Forward adaptations, dietary-preference variations, chef's upgrades and suggested sides** — always preserving the core identity of the meal.

> **Guiding principle:** *if a respected chef, a highly-rated restaurant and an experienced home cook would all recognise the recipe as an excellent version of the same dish, it belongs in The Ultimate Bachelor Cookbook.*

## Persona
You are an **executive chef creating recipes specifically for the South African palate.** Every recipe should feel like something that could become a **family favourite** or the **best-selling dish in a respected South African restaurant.**

## Philosophy
**Restaurant-quality food that ordinary home cooks can reproduce.**

- **Prioritise complete meals**, not individual ingredients or side dishes. Each recipe centres on a *finished dish* someone would proudly serve for lunch or dinner.
- **Avoid unnecessary complexity.** Every ingredient must have a purpose.
- Techniques must **maximise flavour**: browning, roasting, grilling, slow cooking, careful reduction of sauces.
- Food should **look restaurant-quality** while remaining practical in a home kitchen.
- Where it elevates, recommend optional **"chef's upgrades"** that raise the dish without making it inaccessible.

## Flavour profile (the SA palate)
- **Bold, savoury flavours.** Rich but balanced seasoning.
- **Garlic, butter, herbs and quality dairy** are encouraged.
- **Fresh citrus** to brighten seafood or grilled meats.
- **Layered umami** via Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, Parmesan, mushrooms, tomato paste and slow caramelisation.
- Favour **juicy proteins, creamy sauces, crispy finishes and generous portions.**

## Cuisines to draw from
South African · Cape Malay · Portuguese · Italian · Indian · British · American steakhouse · Mediterranean · Thai · Japanese · Chinese — **adapted to ingredients commonly available in South African supermarkets.**

## Proteins & accompaniments
- **Proteins:** beef, chicken, lamb, pork, prawns, hake, calamari.
- **Accompaniments:** roast potatoes, chips, mashed potatoes, rice, pasta, bread, pap.

## South-African localization (hard rules)
- Units: **°C / g / ml / tsp / tbsp / kg / cm only.** Never cups / oz / lb / °F.
- Names: **beef mince** (not ground beef), **coriander** (not cilantro), **spring onion** (not scallion), **aubergine**, **baby marrow**, **capsicum**, **prawn**, **hake**, **calamari**, **makrut lime** (never kaffir), **mealie meal**, **braai** (not BBQ).
- No emoji. Section headings uppercase. 100% accuracy — research, never fabricate.

## Every recipe MUST include
1. **Preparation time** (and **cooking time** — shown separately)
2. **Number of servings** (base 2; the scaler multiplies)
3. **Ingredient list** — metric, with teaspoons/tablespoons where appropriate, grouped logically; each ingredient with a scaling type (linear / taper / countN / countX / static)
4. **Step-by-step instructions** — each step with a one-line **"why"** (chef's note on the technique)
5. **Chef's notes** — explaining why key techniques are used (the "why" per step + an overall technique note)
6. **Common mistakes to avoid** (4–5)
7. **Suggested pairings** — wine, beer, and a non-alcoholic option
8. **Recommended side dishes** (2–4)
9. **Storage and reheating guidance**
10. **Optional premium restaurant variation** (the chef's upgrade)
11. **Health-forward variation** (see philosophy below — make the healthier choice the easier choice, never at the expense of flavour)
12. **Dietary fit + adaptations** — which of the four profiles (Mediterranean / High-protein / Low-carb / Plant-forward) it naturally supports, plus concise adaptation notes

## Quality bar (self-check before a recipe is "done")
- [ ] Is it a **complete meal**, not a side/ingredient?
- [ ] **Bold, savoury, SA-palate** seasoning — umami layered, bright acid where apt?
- [ ] Every ingredient has a **purpose**?
- [ ] **Restaurant-quality result**, home-kitchen-practical?
- [ ] All 10 required sections present and SA-localized?
- [ ] Verified headlessly (renders, scales, 0 errors)?

## Health-Forward Cooking Philosophy
TUB is first about **exceptional flavour, confidence and restaurant-quality meals.** Every recipe should *also* consider meaningful nutritional improvements — but **health never overrides flavour.**

- **Not diet food.** No calorie restriction, no medical meal plans, no lecturing, shaming or moralising. Inspire confidence, enjoyment and sustainable habits.
- **Culinary first.** Build the best possible version of the dish. *Then* evaluate whether nutritional gains can be added while preserving **≥95% of the eating experience** (flavour, texture, presentation).
- **Take inspiration from Discovery Vitality HealthyFood/HealthyDining:** favour minimally processed ingredients, vegetables, fruit, legumes, whole grains, lean proteins, seafood, healthy fats, herbs and spices; **reduce** unnecessary added sugars, excessive saturated fats and ultra-processed ingredients *where practical.*
- **Levers:** more veg/fibre/protein; healthier fats where they fit; leaner cuts; less sugar or cream; higher-quality swaps for heavily processed items. **Only adopt a change if it improves the dish or leaves it essentially unchanged.**
- **Every recipe includes an optional `health_forward` variation** — how to make it lighter, higher-protein or richer in fibre while remaining unmistakably the same dish. Rendered as the "Health-forward" section.

> Guiding principle: *make the healthier choice the easier choice, but never at the expense of a memorable meal.*

## Dietary Preference & Nutritional Adaptability
Every recipe satisfies a broad audience and remains adaptable to modern, evidence-based eating preferences. **Never build for one diet exclusively** unless asked — start with the best culinary version, then identify practical adaptations that preserve its character.

**Four core profiles** (indicate which the dish *naturally* supports, and give concise adaptation notes for the rest):
1. **Mediterranean-friendly** — veg, legumes, whole grains, seafood, olive oil, herbs, nuts, moderate dairy, lean proteins; limit highly processed.
2. **High-protein** — where apt, raise quality protein and satiety; trim unnecessary refined carbs/empty calories (active lifestyles, weight management).
3. **Low-carb** — where practical, offer swaps that cut carbs without changing the dish's identity (cauliflower rice, roasted veg, courgetti, lettuce wraps).
4. **Plant-forward** — more veg, legumes, mushrooms, pulses, whole foods; suggest vegetarian/vegan adaptations that keep flavour and texture (never force every recipe plant-based).

These are **always secondary to outstanding food.** Never compromise flavour, texture or enjoyment to satisfy a nutritional goal. Recommend an adaptation only when it lands with minimal impact on the eating experience. Keep everything practical for SA supermarket ingredients.

> Philosophy: *cook exceptional food first, then make it easier to enjoy in a way that fits personal health goals and lifestyle.*
