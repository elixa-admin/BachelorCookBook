# House Influences & Technique References

> The cooking philosophy and technique DNA this project draws on — captured so recipe
> writing has a consistent backbone. These are *style and principle* references, not
> recipe sources; we write our own. Update as new influences are added.

## What we absorb from each

**Jamie Oliver** — relaxed, fast, deeply accessible. *Take:* speed and one-pan economy
without sacrificing flavour; cook from fresh; treat the reader as capable. Informs the
Solo occasion and ingredient brevity.

**Gordon Ramsay** — precision, seasoning confidence, respect for technique (high heat,
resting meat, built pan sauces). *Take:* the "why" behind each step; sear hot, rest
properly, season boldly. Informs the Date showstoppers and the "the move" tips.

**Anthony Bourdain** — irreverence, ingredient respect, and the romance of cooking.
*Take:* less is more; honour the ingredient and the tradition; never precious, never
snobby. Informs the voice (warm, unpretentious, generous) and the global reach.

**Sip and Feast (Jim Delmage)** — rigorous New York Italian-American home cooking;
detailed, methodical, budget-aware; restaurant-quality achievable at home. *Take:*
respect for tradition and the conviction that great food need not be expensive. Informs
the Italian dishes (cacio e pepe, carbonara, risotto) and the Host occasion's
feeding-a-table generosity.

**Binging with Babish / Basics with Babish (Andrew Rea)** — foundational technique
taught with rigour and warmth; "attempt, often fail, but always teach." *Take:* teach
the building blocks (sear, braise, emulsion, temper, roast) so the reader levels up for
life, not just one dish. Informs the technique-led structure of every recipe and the
"learn once, reuse forever" ethos behind the Signature 12.

## House synthesis

Confident like Ramsay, relaxed like Oliver, romantic like Bourdain, rigorous like Sip
and Feast, foundational like Babish. We keep Bourdain's voice (warm, irreverent,
unpretentious), Babish's teaching spine (every recipe levels up a technique), and the
Sip-and-Feast conviction that proper food is achievable at home on any budget.

## How to use this when writing recipes

- Every recipe teaches **one transferable technique** — state it in "the move".
- Respect the ingredient; don't overcomplicate.
- Be precise where it matters (heat, timing, seasoning), relaxed everywhere else.
- Global, tradition-respecting, budget-honest — never snobby.
