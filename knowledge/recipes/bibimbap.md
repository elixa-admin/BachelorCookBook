# Bibimbap

> A bowl that looks like more work than it is — rice under a wheel of separately seasoned
> vegetables, a fried egg holding the whole thing together, gochujang for heat. Everything
> gets mixed at the table, which is half the theatre. Composed, not chaotic; that's the
> whole trick.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup short-grain rice, cooked
- 200g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 garlic clove, grated (for the beef)
- A large handful of spinach
- 1 carrot, julienned
- 1 baby marrow, julienned
- A handful of beansprouts
- 4 shiitake or button mushrooms, sliced
- 2 eggs
- 2 tbsp gochujang, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp rice vinegar, ½ tsp sugar (sauce)
- Sesame seeds and spring onion, to finish
- Neutral oil, for frying

*Each vegetable is seasoned on its own — don't skip the individual pans, it's the point.*

## Steps

1. Toss the beef in the soy, sesame oil, and garlic; set aside to marinate while you prep.
2. Blanch the spinach 30 seconds, squeeze dry, season with a little sesame oil and salt.
3. Sauté the carrot, baby marrow, mushrooms, and beansprouts separately, each just a minute
   or two, each seasoned with a pinch of salt. Keep them apart.
4. Sear the beef hot and fast, a minute or two, until just cooked.
5. Fry the eggs sunny-side up with crisp, lacy edges.
6. Mix the gochujang, sesame oil, vinegar, and sugar for the sauce.
7. Rice in the bowl, vegetables arranged around the edge, beef in the centre, egg on top.
   Sauce over, sesame seeds and spring onion to finish. Mix everything together at the
   table.

## The move

Season each vegetable in its own pan, one at a time, rather than one big stir-fry. It's
more washing up and it's why bibimbap tastes composed instead of muddy.

## To drink

A crisp lager or soju, cold. Non-alcohol: barley tea or sparkling water with lime.
