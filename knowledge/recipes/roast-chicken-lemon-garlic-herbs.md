# Roast Chicken with Lemon, Garlic & Herbs

> A whole chicken is the most generous thing you can pull out of an oven. Almost no
> hands-on time, a kitchen that smells like Sunday, and a table that suddenly feels like
> an occasion. One bird feeds four; two feed a party.

## Ingredients

- 1 whole chicken, ~1.8kg
- 1 lemon, halved
- 1 head of garlic, halved across the middle
- A bunch of thyme or rosemary
- 60g butter, softened
- Salt and pepper
- Olive oil

*Rule of thumb: one bird per four people.*

## Steps

1. Heat the oven to 220°C.
2. Pat the bird dry. Season the cavity, then stuff it with the lemon, garlic, and herbs.
3. Rub the skin all over with butter and salt generously.
4. Roast 20 minutes at 220°C, then drop to 180°C for about 45 minutes — 15 minutes per
   450g — until the juices run clear from the thigh.
5. Rest under foil for 15 minutes.
6. Carve and serve with the pan juices.

## The move

Dry skin plus a hot start equals crisp. Resting is non-negotiable; a bird carved too soon
bleeds onto the board.

## To drink

A Chardonnay. Non-alc: cloudy apple, gently warmed with a clove.
