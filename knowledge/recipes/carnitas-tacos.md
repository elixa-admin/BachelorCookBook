# Slow-Braised Carnitas Tacos

> Carnitas reward patience: pork cooked in its own fat until it falls apart, then crisped
> at the edges so every taco has soft and crunch. It feeds a table, improves if made
> ahead, and turns any evening into a fiesta.

## Ingredients

- 1.5kg pork shoulder, cubed
- 1 orange, juiced, plus the squeezed halves
- 1 onion, quartered
- 4 garlic cloves
- 1 tsp oregano
- Salt
- A little lard or oil
- Corn tortillas, salsa, lime, cilantro, to serve

*Per serving: ~250g pork.*

## Steps

1. In a heavy pot, combine the pork, onion, garlic, oregano, salt, orange juice and the
   squeezed halves. Add water to barely cover.
2. Simmer uncovered about 2 hours, until the liquid reduces right down and the pork is
   tender.
3. Raise the heat and let the pork fry in its own rendered fat, turning, until the edges
   crisp.
4. Shred with two forks, keeping plenty of crispy bits.
5. Pile into warm tortillas with salsa, lime, and cilantro.

## The move

Two textures on purpose — shred the tender pork, then let some of it crisp in the fat.
That contrast is the whole dish.

## To drink

A margarita or a Mexican lager. Non-alc: lime soda with a salt rim.
