# Korean BBQ Beef

> Bulgogi-style beef, seared hot and fast, wrapped in lettuce at the table — it's
> interactive by design, which does half your hosting for you. The marinade needs thirty
> minutes minimum, so start it before anything else and the rest of the evening is easy.

## Ingredients

- 300g beef sirloin, sliced thin against the grain
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 garlic cloves, grated
- 1cm ginger, grated
- ¼ pear or apple, grated
- 2 spring onions, sliced
- Lettuce leaves, steamed rice, and ssamjang or gochujang, to serve
- Sesame seeds, to finish

*Scales directly with the beef — keep the marinade ratios the same and multiply the
meat by the head count.*

## Steps

1. Mix the soy, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and grated pear. Toss the beef through
   and marinate at least 30 minutes — overnight if you're planning ahead.
2. Get a pan very hot. Sear the beef in batches, a minute or two a side — don't crowd the
   pan or it'll steam instead of char.
3. Scatter with spring onion and sesame seeds.
4. Serve with lettuce leaves, rice, and ssamjang for wrapping at the table.

## The move

The grated pear isn't for sweetness — its enzymes tenderise the beef in the marinade,
which is why a cheap, thin-sliced cut ends up this good.

## To drink

A cold Korean lager or soju. Non-alcohol: chilled barley tea.
