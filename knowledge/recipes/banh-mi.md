# Banh Mi

> A proper sandwich, built the way a sandwich should be — crackling bread, something
> seared and hot, something quick-pickled and sharp, herbs to finish. Fifteen minutes of
> actual effort, eaten standing at the counter or taken to the table like it matters
> either way.

## Ingredients

- ½ carrot, julienned
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar, 1 tsp sugar, a pinch of salt (for the quick pickle)
- 1 chicken thigh or 100g pork, sliced thin
- 1 tsp five-spice, 1 tsp soy sauce
- 1 baguette roll or crusty sub roll
- Mayonnaise
- Pâté, if you have it — good, not essential
- ¼ cucumber, sliced thin
- Coriander, sliced chilli, spring onion

*A single-serving sandwich by nature — double everything for two, but pickle and sear
in the same pan regardless.*

## Steps

1. Toss the carrot in the vinegar, sugar, and salt. Set aside — even 15 minutes turns it
   properly crisp and tangy.
2. Toss the meat in the five-spice and soy. Sear hot, a few minutes a side, until cooked
   through and a little charred at the edges.
3. Split and lightly toast the roll.
4. Spread with mayonnaise and pâté if using. Layer the meat, pickled carrot, cucumber,
   coriander, chilli, and spring onion.

## The move

Pickle the carrot first, before anything else goes near the pan. Even a short soak in
vinegar and sugar is the difference between a sandwich and a Bánh mì.

## To drink

Vietnamese iced coffee, strong and sweet. It works standing at the counter as well as it
does anywhere else.
