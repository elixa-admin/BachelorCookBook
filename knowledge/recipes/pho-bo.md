# Pho Bo

> Real pho simmers for the better part of a day; this is the honest weeknight version —
> charred aromatics and toasted spice steeped hard into good stock, ready in under an hour.
> Paper-thin beef goes in raw, and the broth itself does the cooking, right at the table.

## Ingredients

- 1 onion, halved, 1 knob ginger, halved lengthways
- 2 star anise, 1 cinnamon stick, 3 cloves
- 1L good beef stock
- 2 tbsp fish sauce, 1 tsp sugar
- 200g beef sirloin, sliced paper-thin (easiest when half-frozen)
- 200g flat rice noodles
- Beansprouts, spring onion, coriander, lime, chilli, to serve

*Doubling the stock quantities scales this cleanly; the beef and noodles scale per
person as written.*

## Steps

1. Char the onion and ginger — directly over a flame or in a dry, hot pan — until
   blackened in patches. This is where the depth comes from.
2. Toast the star anise, cinnamon, and cloves in the empty pot for a minute until fragrant.
3. Add the stock, charred onion and ginger, fish sauce, and sugar. Simmer 30 minutes.
   Strain and keep hot.
4. Cook the noodles according to the packet; divide between bowls with beansprouts and
   raw beef laid on top.
5. Ladle the scalding broth over the beef — it should cook through in seconds. Finish with
   spring onion, coriander, lime, and chilli.

## The move

Slice the beef as thin as you can manage, then trust the broth: pour it over raw and let
the heat do the cooking. Cook the beef in the pot first and you lose the point of the dish.

## To drink

A light Vietnamese lager or a dry Riesling. Non-alcohol: iced coriander and lime soda.
