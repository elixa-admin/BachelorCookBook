# Risotto Milanese with Saffron

> Risotto asks for twenty minutes of your attention and gives back something golden and
> soothing. Stand at the stove, add the stock slowly, and stir — it's the rare dish that
> improves the more present you are.

## Ingredients

- 150g carnaroli or arborio rice
- 700ml hot stock
- A pinch of saffron, steeped in 2 tbsp hot stock
- 1 shallot, diced
- 50ml white wine (or extra stock)
- 50g butter
- 50g parmesan, grated
- Salt

*Per serving: 75g rice.*

## Steps

1. Sweat the shallot in half the butter — soft, not coloured.
2. Add the rice and toast it 2 minutes, until the edges turn translucent.
3. Pour in the wine; stir until absorbed.
4. Add the hot stock a ladle at a time, stirring and waiting until each is absorbed
   before the next — about 18 minutes.
5. Stir in the saffron and its soaking liquid; cook 2 minutes more.
6. Off the heat, beat in the rest of the butter and the parmesan until glossy. Rest 1
   minute before serving.

## The move

The final beat off the heat — butter and cheese whipped in — is what makes the risotto
wave on the plate instead of sitting there.

## To drink

The dry white you cooked with. Non-alc: an alcohol-free white.
