# Bouillabaisse

> This is Marseille in a bowl — saffron and fennel, orange peel and garlic, a broth built
> slow then finished in minutes flat. It looks like restaurant theatre because it is: you
> ladle it out at the table, rouille melting into the broth, and nobody needs to know it's
> mostly a good stock and good timing.

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 fennel bulb, sliced, fronds reserved
- 1 leek, sliced
- 3 garlic cloves, sliced
- 2 tomatoes, chopped (or 1 × 400g tin)
- A generous pinch of saffron
- A strip of orange zest
- 100ml dry white wine
- 500ml good fish or seafood stock
- 300g firm white fish (hake or kingklip), cut into chunks
- 200g mussels, cleaned
- 8 prawns, shell-on
- Mayonnaise, garlic, and cayenne, for a quick rouille
- Crusty bread, toasted

*Scales cleanly by the seafood: keep roughly 250g mixed seafood per person.*

## Steps

1. Soften the fennel and leek in the oil, 8 minutes, until sweet but not coloured.
2. Add the garlic, tomatoes, saffron, and orange zest. Cook 2 minutes until fragrant.
3. Pour in the wine, let it bubble away, then add the stock. Simmer 15 minutes to let the
   flavours marry.
4. Slide in the firm fish first; poach 3 minutes. Add the mussels and prawns; cover and
   cook 4–5 minutes more, until the mussels open and the prawns turn pink.
5. Whip a quick rouille: mayonnaise, a crushed garlic clove, a pinch of cayenne, a few
   saffron threads bloomed in a spoon of warm broth.
6. Ladle into bowls over toasted bread. Rouille on the side, fennel fronds scattered over.

## The move

Build the broth first, seafood last, and in stages — firm fish before shellfish, shellfish
before anything delicate. Dump it all in at once and half of it overcooks before the rest
is done.

## To drink

A cold Provençal rosé or a dry Muscadet. Non-alcohol: soda water with a wide strip of
orange peel.
