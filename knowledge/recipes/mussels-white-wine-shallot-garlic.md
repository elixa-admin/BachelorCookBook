# Mussels in White Wine, Shallot & Garlic

> Mussels are the best-kept secret of cooking for two: cheap as chips, on the table in
> fifteen minutes, and dramatic enough to feel like an event. The broth does the work —
> all you need is good bread and someone to share it with.

## Ingredients

- 1 kg mussels, scrubbed and debearded
- 1 shallot, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, sliced
- 150ml dry white wine
- 40g butter
- A handful of flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Crusty bread, to serve

*Scales by the kilo: ~500g mussels per person.*

## Steps

1. Tap any open mussels; discard the ones that refuse to close.
2. Melt the butter and sweat the shallot and garlic — soft, not browned, about 3 minutes.
3. Tip in the mussels and wine. Cover, high heat, 4–5 minutes, giving the pot one shake.
4. The moment the shells gape open, they're done. Discard any that stay shut.
5. Off the heat, fold in the parsley. Serve in deep bowls with bread to mop the broth.

## The move

The broth is the point. Don't overcook — the second the shells open, they're finished.

## To drink

The same dry white you cooked with — Muscadet or Picpoul. Non-alc: a crisp alcohol-free
white, well chilled.
