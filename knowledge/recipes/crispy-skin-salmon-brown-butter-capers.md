# Crispy-Skin Salmon, Brown Butter & Capers

> Get the skin crisp and you've won the night. The trick is a cold pan, patience, and
> letting the fillets speak for themselves — brown butter and capers finish the job.
> Fifteen minutes, and it plates like a restaurant.

## Ingredients

- 2 salmon fillets, skin on, ~180g each
- Flaky salt
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 60g butter
- 1 tbsp capers, drained
- 1 lemon

*Per serving: 1 fillet, ~30g butter.*

## Steps

1. Pat the skin bone-dry; salt it.
2. Lay the fillets skin-down in a cold, oiled pan, then set it over medium heat. Press
   them flat for the first minute so they don't curl.
3. Render about 6 minutes, until the skin is golden and crisp. Flip, 2 minutes more for
   a just-translucent centre.
4. Lift the fish out. In the same pan, melt the butter until it smells nutty and goes
   brown, then add the capers and a squeeze of lemon.
5. Spoon the brown butter over the fish and serve.

## The move

Cold pan, then heat. It renders the skin slowly so it crisps instead of buckling.

## To drink

A Pinot Noir, or a gin and tonic. Non-alc: tonic with a ribbon of cucumber.
