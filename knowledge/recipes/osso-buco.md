# Osso Buco

> Ask the butcher for osso buco-cut beef shin, bone-in — then let the oven do the work for
> three unhurried hours while the kitchen fills with the smell of it. This is the "cooked
> all day" flex with about twenty minutes of actual effort, finished with a bright,
> last-minute gremolata that cuts straight through the richness.

## Ingredients

- 2 beef shin pieces, osso buco-cut, bone-in
- Cake flour, for dusting
- Olive oil
- 1 onion, 1 carrot, 1 celery stick, all finely diced
- 2 garlic cloves, chopped
- 150ml white wine
- 1 × 400g tin tomatoes
- 300ml beef stock
- 1 bay leaf
- Zest of 1 lemon, 1 garlic clove (extra), a handful of parsley, chopped — for the gremolata

*Scales cleanly per shank: one shank per person, browned and braised the same way
regardless of quantity.*

## Steps

1. Heat the oven to 160°C. Dust the shanks in flour, season well.
2. Sear hard in the oil on all sides in a dutch oven until deeply browned. Remove.
3. Soften the onion, carrot, celery, and garlic in the same pot, scraping up the browned
   bits.
4. Deglaze with the wine. Add the tomatoes, stock, bay leaf, and the shanks. Bring to a
   simmer.
5. Cover and braise in the oven 2.5–3 hours, until the meat is falling from the bone.
6. Mix the gremolata — lemon zest, garlic, parsley — and scatter it over just before
   serving.

## The move

The gremolata goes on at the very last second, off the heat. It's a raw, bright finish
against hours of slow braising — add it any earlier and the heat kills it.

## To drink

A Nebbiolo or a Chianti. Non-alcohol: sparkling grape juice with a twist of lemon.
