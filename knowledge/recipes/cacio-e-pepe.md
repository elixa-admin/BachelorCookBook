# Cacio e Pepe

> Three ingredients and a technique — that's the whole dish. Get the emulsion right and
> you've got the silkiest pasta in Rome; rush it and you've got cheese clumps. Worth
> slowing down for.

## Ingredients

- 200g tonnarelli or spaghetti
- 60g pecorino romano, finely grated
- 1 heaped tsp black peppercorns, freshly cracked
- Salt (for the water)

*Per serving: 100g pasta, 30g pecorino.*

## Steps

1. Toast the cracked pepper in a dry pan for a minute, until it smells warm.
2. Cook the pasta in well-salted water until al dente. Reserve a mug of the cloudy water
   before draining.
3. Off the heat, add the pasta to the pepper with a splash of the pasta water and most of
   the pecorino. Toss vigorously, adding water gradually until it turns glossy.
4. Finish with the rest of the cheese and more pepper.

## The move

The starchy pasta water is the emulsifier. Add it gradually and toss hard — you want
gloss, not soup.

## To drink

A crisp white or a cold lager. Non-alc: sparkling water with lemon.
