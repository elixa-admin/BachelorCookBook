# Moussaka

> Layers of aubergine and spiced mince under a blanket of béchamel, baked until the top is
> freckled gold. It takes an hour of mostly unattended oven time, and it tastes like
> Sunday in Athens — the kind of dish you can build ahead and reheat, so all the effort
> happens before she arrives.

## Ingredients

- 2 aubergines, sliced into rounds, salted 20 minutes and patted dry
- Olive oil
- 1 onion, diced, 2 garlic cloves, chopped
- 300g beef mince
- 1 × 400g tin tomatoes
- 1 tsp cinnamon, 1 tsp dried oregano
- 100ml red wine
- 40g butter, 40g cake flour, 400ml milk (béchamel)
- A grating of nutmeg
- 1 egg yolk, 30g grated hard cheese

*Scales by the layer: one more aubergine and a third more mince comfortably stretches
this to four.*

## Steps

1. Brush the aubergine slices with oil and roast or pan-fry until golden on both sides.
2. Soften the onion and garlic, then brown the mince. Add the tomatoes, cinnamon, oregano,
   and wine; simmer 15 minutes until thick.
3. Make the béchamel: melt the butter, stir in the flour, cook a minute, then whisk in the
   milk gradually until smooth and thick. Season with nutmeg and salt. Off the heat,
   temper in the egg yolk and cheese.
4. Layer in a baking dish: aubergine, mince, aubergine, then béchamel over the top.
5. Bake at 180°C for 35–40 minutes until deeply golden.
6. Rest 10–15 minutes before cutting — non-negotiable.

## The move

Rest it properly before you cut it, the same as a lasagne. Skip the rest and the layers
collapse into something that tastes just as good but looks like nothing at all.

## To drink

A Greek Xinomavro or a Malbec. Non-alcohol: sparkling water with a wide strip of lemon.
