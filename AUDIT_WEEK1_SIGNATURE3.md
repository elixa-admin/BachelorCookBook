# RECIPE 1: Risotto Milanese with Saffron

## Current TUB Version Analysis

### Current Lead (From tub-app.html):
> "This is risotto as the Milanese intend: not stand-and-stir for twenty minutes, but a smarter method that rinses rice with stock to capture its starch, then toasts it for nuttiness before a gentle simmer. Saffron threads — just two generous pinches — bloom in the hot broth, tinting the rice daffodil-yellow and infusing it with that singular earthy, honeyed aroma. Finished with butter and Parmigiano-Reggiano off the heat, the grains should be tender but al dente at the core, swimming in a creamy sauce that flows like lava."

### Current Ingredients (From recipes-data.js):
- **The rice:** 400g Carnaroli or Vialone Nano rice
- **Stock:** 950ml Chicken or vegetable stock
- **Oil:** 45ml Extra-virgin olive oil
- **Aromatics:** 1 small yellow onion (minced), 225ml dry white wine, 2 pinches saffron threads, 1 tsp flaky sea salt
- **Finish:** 30g unsalted butter, 40g Parmigiano-Reggiano (finely grated)

### Current Technique (Steps):
1. **Rinse the rice** — Agitate in stock to release surface starch; drain and save starchy liquid
2. **Toast the rice** — Heat oil to shimmering; add drained rice; cook 5 min until sizzling and nutty
3. **Sauté aromatics** — Add minced onion; cook 1 min; add wine; cook until pan nearly dry
4. **Add saffron stock** — Pour in stock + saffron; bring to simmer; cover; reduce heat to lowest setting
5. **Simmer gently** — Cook covered 10 min undisturbed; uncover, stir, redistribute; cover and cook 5 more min
6. **Finish to creaminess** — Stir in reserved stock + butter; cook until creamy; add cheese; stir rapidly off heat

**Current timing: 25 min active**

# RECIPE 2: Cacio e Pepe

## Current Analysis

### Current Lead:
> "Rome's minimalist masterpiece: spaghetti tossed with Pecorino Romano and black pepper into a creamy emulsion. No cream, no eggs, no shortcuts — just pasta water, cheese, and pepper."

### Current Ingredients:
- **Pasta:** 400g spaghetti (or bucatini)
- **Cheese:** 150g Pecorino Romano (finely grated)
- **Pepper:** 2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- **Salt:** 1 tsp flaky sea salt

### Current Technique:
1. Bring large pot of salted water to rolling boil
2. Add pasta; cook until al dente
3. Reserve 500ml pasta water
4. Toast pepper in large bowl until aromatic
5. Add a splash of pasta water to the pepper
6. Add hot pasta to bowl
7. Toss constantly, adding pasta water as needed, until creamy
8. Add cheese gradually, stirring rapidly
9. Serve immediately with more cheese

**Current timing: 18 min**

### Critical Technique: The Emulsion

**Keller's approach:** Add pasta water sparingly, whisk constantly, create a *water-cheese-starch emulsion*. Never let it get too wet or too dry.

**Pépin's approach:** Add cold cheese by handfuls, stir each handful in completely before adding next. Pasta water is the binder, not the main component.

**Ramsay's approach:** Work FAST. Add cheese, add water, toss aggressively. The motion matters as much as the ingredients.

**Consensus:** Cheese must be cold, pasta water starch is the emulsifier, and the process must be quick and constant-motion.

**Current TUB:** Missing the *speed* emphasis and the *cold cheese* requirement.

# RECIPE 3: Pan-Seared Steak

## Current Analysis

### Current Lead:
> "A great steak is less recipe than ritual. Salt it early, let the pan get properly hot, baste in foaming butter and thyme. Cook it for someone you'd like to keep talking to."

### Current Ingredients:
- **Steak:** 1 × 300–350g ribeye or ny strip (1.5 inches thick)
- **Salt & pepper:** Flaky sea salt, freshly ground black pepper
- **Fat for pan:** 30ml neutral oil (or clarified butter)
- **Finish:** 30g unsalted butter, fresh thyme

### Current Technique:
1. Bring steak to room temperature (20 min)
2. Pat thoroughly dry
3. Season generously with salt 40 min before cooking
4. Heat pan until very hot (oil should shimmer/smoke)
5. Sear 2.5 min per side (1.5" steak)
6. Add butter + thyme; baste while finishing
7. Check internal temp: 52–54°C for medium-rare
8. Rest 5 min on warm plate

**Current timing: 25 min (including rest)**

### Critical Keller Principle: Temperature Precision

| Temp | Keller's Definition | How It Eats |
|------|-------------------|-----------|
| 50–52°C | **Very rare** | Cool center, blood-rare |
| **52–54°C** | **Medium-rare (CANON)** | Warm center, rosy, just set |
| 55–57°C | Medium | Light pink, fully set |
| 58–60°C | Medium-well | Just pink at center |

**Keller's non-negotiable rule:** Remove steak at 52°C (so it coasts to 54°C during rest). This is the moment where you have the most margin for error.

**Current TUB:** Says "52–54°C" which is correct, but doesn't explain the "remove early and let it coast" logic.
